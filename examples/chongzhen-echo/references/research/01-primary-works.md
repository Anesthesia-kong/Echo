# Chongzhen Emperor 崇禎 (Zhu Youjian 朱由檢, 1611–1644) — Surviving Primary Works

**Reign**: Chongzhen 1 – Chongzhen 17 (1627–1644, lunar) / Feb 1628 – Apr 1644 Gregorian.
**Death**: hanged himself on a sophora (pagoda) tree at Wansui Shan 萬歲山 / "Coal Hill" 煤山 (later Jingshan 景山) on Chongzhen 17/3/19 = 25 April 1644 (Gregorian).
**Purpose of this document**: compile the authentic written voice of Zhu Youjian — edicts, rescripts, poems, calligraphy — with authenticity labels and variant preservation. Feeds the Echo distillation.

**Authenticity labels used throughout**:
- `[documented]` — survives in a primary Ming-era document contemporaneous with issue.
- `[attested]` — recorded by a named late-Ming eyewitness / near-contemporary (Ji Liuqi, Tan Qian, Wang Shide, Yang Shicong, Feng Menglong) within ~20 years.
- `[attributed]` — transmitted under his name in a reliable Qing compilation (明史 1739, 池北偶談 c.1691) but ~50–100 years after death.
- `[legendary]` — circulates in popular historiography or fiction without stable textual anchor.
- `[modern-scholarly-inference]` — conclusion reached by 19th–21st-c. scholars, not a Ming/Qing source.

**Key textual problem**: the full 《崇禎實錄》 (Veritable Records of the Chongzhen Reign) was destroyed in 1644 when Li Zicheng's forces entered Beijing. What we call 《崇禎長編》 is a Qing-era reconstruction from salvaged fragments (66 juan survive; a two-juan 痛史 remnant 崇禎長編 also circulates). Therefore even the most "official" surviving record of his own voice has passed through Qing editorial hands. Treat every quotation as mediated unless flagged otherwise.

---

## 1. What survives vs. what is lost

### LOST
- 《崇禎實錄》 in its original Ming-compiled form — burned/dispersed 1644.
- The great bulk of routine 批紅 / 硃批 (vermillion rescripts) — only scattered excerpts transmitted through private histories.
- The text, if any, actually written on his garment on 1644/3/19 — physical evidence perished with his corpse; what circulates is reported testimony.
- Any substantial body of original poetry (he was not a poet-emperor in the Huizong/Qianlong mould; his output is tiny).

### SURVIVES (in mediated form)
- **Six 罪己詔 (self-blaming edicts)** — partial Chinese texts via 《崇禎長編》 fragments, 《國榷》 (Tan Qian 談遷), 《明季北略》 (Ji Liuqi 計六奇), and 《明實錄》 reconstructions.
- **The Yuan Chonghuan indictment** (1630/8) — preserved verbatim in 《崇禎長編》 juan 36 and reproduced in 《明史》 juan 259 袁崇煥傳.
- **Wei Zhongxian purge edict** (1627/11) — preserved in 《崇禎長編》 fragments and 《明史》 305 宦官傳.
- **Five recensions of the suicide/final edict** — Ji Liuqi, Feng Menglong, 明史, popular conflated, and the expanded "文武可殺勿傷陵寢百姓" version.
- **Yang Shicong's denunciation** of street-circulated fabrications in 《甲申核真略》.
- **Four 七絕 to Qin Liangyu** (1630) — transmitted in 王士禎 《池北偶談》 and 王世德 《崇禎遺錄》, with variant readings.
- **Two extant autograph calligraphies**: 《九思》 and 《思無邪》 (Palace Museum / National Palace Museum Taipei lineage; both are *copied* Confucian aphorisms, not original composition).
- **Scattered 批示 one-liners** — quoted in private histories, often in the format "上怒曰 / 上批曰…".
- **Correspondence fragments on the 南遷 debate** with Li Mingrui 李明睿 (Jan 1644) — preserved in 《國榷》 and 《綏寇紀略》.

## 2. The Six 罪己詔 (Self-Blaming Edicts)

Chongzhen issued self-blaming edicts more frequently than any other Ming emperor (traditionally counted as six; some modern counts allow five depending on whether the 1644/3/18 親征詔 is classed as a 罪己詔 or a mobilization edict). Texts below preserved as transmitted; none is fully complete in a single source.

### 2.1 — Chongzhen 8 / October 1635 (after Li Zicheng / Gao Yingxiang's 1635/1 sack of Fengyang 鳳陽 and burning of the Ming ancestral tombs 皇陵)

**Source**: 《崇禎長編》 juan 100; excerpted in 《國榷》 juan 95. [documented — fragmentary]

**Key surviving Chinese text** (as transmitted; converging across 崇禎長編 citations):

> 「朕以藐躬，纘承大統。意與天下更新，用還祖宗之舊。不期倚任非人，遂致虜猖寇起。夫建州本我屬夷，流氛原吾赤子。若使撫御得宜，何敢逆我顏行。以全盛之天下，文武之多人，無奈誇詐得人，實功罕覯。虜乃三入，寇則七年，師徒暴露，黎庶顛連。國帑匱絀而徵調未已，閭閻雕攰而加派難停。中夜思惟，業已不勝愧憤……」

**English rendering** (working translation; no single authoritative published translation — Wakeman *Great Enterprise* vol. 1 paraphrases but does not give a full translation):

> "With My frail person I have inherited the great succession. I intended to renew All-Under-Heaven and restore the old ways of the ancestors. Unexpectedly those I relied on and appointed were unworthy, and so the Tartars grew audacious and the bandits rose up. The Jianzhou were originally my submitted barbarians; the roaming hordes were originally my red children [common people]. Had rule been fitting, how would they have dared defy my countenance? With the fullness of All-Under-Heaven, and so many civil and military men, yet alas — boastful deception has won promotion while real merit is rarely met. The Tartars have thrice invaded; the bandits have endured seven years; the armies are exposed to the elements, the common folk are displaced. The treasury is empty yet levies never cease; the hamlets are scraped bare yet surtaxes cannot be halted. In the middle of the night I reflect, and am already overwhelmed with shame and indignation…"

### 2.2 — Chongzhen 10 / Intercalary 4th month, 1637 (after prolonged drought and locust plague; the 北方大旱)

**Source**: 《崇禎長編》; 《明史》 本紀 24; quoted in popular summaries. [documented — only fragments survive]

**Key transmitted fragment**:

> 「勳戚不知厭足，縱貪橫於京畿；鄉宦滅棄防維，肆侵凌於閭里。」
> (Meritorious relatives know no sufficiency — they indulge greed and excess in the capital region. Local gentry abandon the restraints — they freely oppress in the hamlets.)

This 1637 edict is the one that most explicitly blames **officials and gentry rather than Heaven** — it is the source text for the recurring theme "諸臣誤朕" avant-la-lettre.

### 2.3 — Chongzhen 15 / 1642 (after Li Zicheng's third siege and the flooding of Kaifeng 開封; also Hong Chengchou's surrender to the Qing at Song-Jin 松錦)

**Source**: 《崇禎長編》; 《國榷》 juan 98; 《明季北略》 juan 18. [documented — fragmentary]

Core transmitted phrase: 「罪在朕躬，勿敢自寬。」 (The blame lies in My person; I dare not give Myself any slack.)

### 2.4 — Chongzhen 16 / 6th month 1643

**Source**: 《崇禎長編》 as reconstructed. [documented]

Full text nearly identical to 2.1 (1635). Two scholarly explanations: (a) Chongzhen literally recycled his own earlier edict language; (b) Later compilers conflated the two. **Preserve the contradiction.**

### 2.5 — Chongzhen 17 / 3rd month 18th day 1644 (親征詔)

**Source**: 《崇禎長編》 痛史本 fragments; 《國榷》 juan 100; 《明季北略》 juan 20. [documented]

> 「朕將親率六師出討，留東宮監國，國家重務，悉以付之。……有能奮發忠勇，或助糧草器械，騾馬舟車，悉詣軍前聽用，以殲醜類。分茅胙土之賞，決不食言！」

### 2.6 — Final edict (3/19/1644) — see Section 3 below

## 3. The Suicide Edict — Five Recensions

### 3.1 — Ji Liuqi 計六奇, 《明季北略》 juan 20 (earliest stabilized, c.1670)

[attested — earliest full textual witness, ~26 years after the event]

> 「朕自登極十七年，致敵入內地四次，逆賊直逼京師。雖朕薄德匪躬，上干天咎，然皆諸臣之誤朕也。朕死無面目見祖宗於地下。去朕冠冕，以髮覆面，任賊分裂朕屍，勿傷百姓一人。」

### 3.2 — Feng Menglong 馮夢龍, 《甲申紀事》 / 《燕都日記》 (c.1644–45)

[attested — closest temporally but editorially shaped]

Retains the "諸臣誤朕" + "勿傷百姓一人" kernel; varies "薄德匪躬" vs. "涼德藐躬", "分裂朕屍" vs. "裂朕屍".

### 3.3 — 《明史》 juan 24 思宗本紀 (Qing 1739)

[attributed — ~95 years after death]

Smoothed language. Opens **「朕涼德藐躬，上干天咎」**. This is the version most often cited in modern Chinese textbooks.

### 3.4 — Popular conflated version (Qing-Republican storytelling / stage drama)

[legendary — composite]

> 「朕涼德藐躬，上干天咎，然皆諸臣之誤朕。朕死無面目見祖宗於地下，自去冠冕，以髮覆面。任賊分裂朕屍，勿傷百姓一人。」

### 3.5 — Expanded version with 「文武盡可殺，勿壞陵寢，勿傷百姓」

[legendary — rhetorical amplification]

Adds clause empowering Li Zicheng to execute all civil and military officials while protecting imperial tombs and commoners. No early witness carries this phrase.

### 3.6 — Yang Shicong 楊士聰 《甲申核真略》 — the denunciation

[attested — the authoritative skeptical voice]

Yang Shicong was in Beijing through the fall. He writes that:
- The emperor's actual final written trace was **minimal** — possibly just "因失江山，無面目見祖宗，不敢終於正寢" (two short lines).
- The dramatic "任賊分裂朕屍" / "寧裂朕屍" additions are **"淺人憤詞" (shallow men's indignant phrases) — not the emperor's actual words**.
- 趙士錦 reported that what was found on the body was only the two characters **「天子」** written on his left hand/sleeve, possibly by the accompanying eunuch 王承恩 so that looters could identify the body.

**This is the single most important skeptical witness. Preserve all five variants; preserve Yang's denunciation.**

## 4. The Yuan Chonghuan 袁崇煥 Indictment (1630/8/16)

**Sources**: 《崇禎長編》 juan 36; 《明史》 juan 259 袁崇煥傳. [documented]

> 「諭以袁崇煥付託不效，專恃欺隱。以市米則資盜，以謀款則斬帥。縱敵長驅，頓兵不戰。援兵四集，盡行遣散。及兵薄城下，又潛攜喇嘛，堅請入城。種種罪惡，命刑部會官磔示。依律磔之，家屬十六歲以上處斬，十五歲以下給功臣家為奴。」

This is the most verbatim-stable long text of his voice. The phrase **"付託不效，專恃欺隱"** — "entrusted yet ineffective, relying on deception" — is the keystone of his political vocabulary.

## 5. Other Major Edicts

### 5.1 — Wei Zhongxian 魏忠賢 purge (1627/11)

**Sources**: 《崇禎長編》 fragments; 《明史》 juan 305 宦官傳. [documented — fragmentary]

Sequence: Qian Jiazheng 錢嘉徵 impeachment read aloud in Wei's presence → demotion to Fengyang → arrest order when Wei traveled south with 1,000 guards → Wei hanged himself at Fucheng 阜城.

Recurring vocabulary: **盜竊國柄 / 擅作威福 / 欺君罔上** — boilerplate across later prosecutions.

### 5.2 — Chen Xinjia 陳新甲 repudiation (1642/9)

[documented — via 《明史》 juan 257 陳新甲傳]

Chen was secretly authorized to pursue peace negotiations with the Qing. When a dispatch leaked, Chongzhen publicly repudiated Chen rather than own his authorization, executed him 1642/9/22.

One-line rescript rejecting Chen's self-defense: **「陳新甲身任本兵，失機陷城，何辭可解？」** ("Chen Xinjia holds the office of War Minister — to lose the strategic moment and allow cities to fall — what words can excuse this?") [attested]

### 5.3 — 1629 Postal-Relay abolition (裁撤驛遞)

[documented via 《崇禎長編》]

Chongzhen 2/5/1629: downsized the postal-relay system. Laid off the courier **Li Zicheng 李自成**, who would overthrow the dynasty.

### 5.4 — 1644 南遷 debate with Li Mingrui 李明睿

[attested — 《國榷》 juan 100; 《綏寇紀略》]

> 「朕有此志久矣，無人贊襄，故遲至今。汝意與朕合。但外邊諸臣不從，奈何？此事重大，爾且密之。」

The rare glimpse of his unguarded private political voice. Pattern: (a) claim prior intent, (b) blame ministers for blocking, (c) demand secrecy, (d) fail to act.

## 6. Marginal Rescripts 批示

Representative examples (all [attested] through private-history transmission):

- **「諸臣誤朕」** — recurring formula, not a one-off.
- **「此輩平日侈口高談，臨事一籌莫展。」** — "These sorts, in normal days full of inflated grand talk, when affairs come not one stratagem to display."
- **「文官個個可殺」** — "Every civil official could be killed." [attested — Wang Shide 王世德 《崇禎遺錄》]
- **「朕非亡國之君，諸臣盡亡國之臣」** — [attested but floating — no single primary source]
- **「陳新甲身任本兵，失機陷城，何辭可解？」**

**Stylistic fingerprint**: short parallel clauses; pivot from self-abasement to minister-blame in a single breath; heavy use of "何" interrogatives; classical allusions sparing but pointed.

## 7. Calligraphy — The "Copied Aphorisms" Finding

### 7.1 — 《九思》 (Nine Thoughts)

Large-character 大楷 in the manner of Yan Zhenqing. Text is a direct citation of 《論語·季氏》:

> 「孔子曰：君子有九思。視思明，聽思聰，色思溫，貌思恭，言思忠，事思敬，疑思問，忿思難，見得思義。」

### 7.2 — 《思無邪》

Three-character phrase from 《論語·為政》 (itself a quotation of 《詩經》 《魯頌·駉》).

### The finding

**Both extant autograph calligraphies are copied Confucian self-correctional aphorisms. There is no surviving specimen of original prose or verse calligraphically brushed as free creative act.**

Compared to Huizong, Qianlong, Kangxi — Chongzhen's visual-artistic self-expression is constrained entirely to citation of Confucian moral-surveillance aphorisms. Both are templates for ruler-self-policing. [modern-scholarly-inference] consistent with the personality pattern in all surviving edicts.

## 8. Poems to Qin Liangyu 秦良玉 (1630) — 《賜秦良玉詩四首》

**Sources**: 王士禎 《池北偶談》 (c.1691); 王世德 《崇禎遺錄》. [attributed — transmission is late and unstable; "為百年未有定本"]

### Poem 1
> 學就西川八陣圖，鴛鴦袖裏握兵符。
> 由來巾幗甘心受，何必將軍是丈夫。

### Poem 2
> 蜀錦征袍自剪成，桃花馬上請長纓。
> 世間多少奇男子，誰肯沙場萬里行。

### Poem 3
> 露宿風餐誓不辭，嘔將鮮血代胭脂。
> 北來高唱勤王曲，不是昭君出塞時。

### Poem 4
> 憑將箕箒作蝥弧，一片歡聲動地呼。
> 試看他年麟閣上，丹青先畫美人圖。

If authentic, these are his only surviving poetry. Either way, preserve all four.

## 9. Recurring Themes (≥3 attestations across corpus)

| Theme / Formula | Attestations | Label |
|---|---|---|
| **諸臣誤朕** | 1643 罪己詔; 1644/3/19 遺詔 (all five); scattered rescripts | [documented + attested] |
| **涼德 / 薄德 / 菲躬 / 匪躬 / 藐躬** | 1635, 1637, 1642, 1643, 1644/3/19 — opens almost every 罪己詔 | [documented] |
| **宵旰靡寧 / 憂勤惕勵** | 1635 and 1643 edicts; echoed in rescripts | [documented] |
| **虜 + 寇 doubled** | 1635 edict ("虜乃三入，寇則七年"); 1643 edict identical; Yuan Chonghuan indictment | [documented] |
| **祖宗 / 社稷 / 百姓 triadic** | All suicide-edict recensions; 1635 edict; 親征詔 | [documented + attested] |
| **勿傷百姓一人** | All five suicide-edict recensions except Yang's skeptical reconstruction | [attested — but suspect per Yang] |
| **上干天咎** | 1635, 1643, 1644/3/19; 1637 locust context | [documented] |
| **付託不效，專恃欺隱** | Yuan Chonghuan indictment; template in Chen Xinjia rescripts | [documented] |

**Pattern-level finding**: the **自責-他咎 pivot** ("self-blame that pivots to other-blame") is the structural signature of almost every surviving Chongzhen text. Edicts open with 涼德藐躬, then pivot at 不期 / 然皆 / 無奈 to blame appointees. This is *stylistic*, not incidental. Most reliable voice-marker for distilling his Echo.

## 10. Modern Misattributions to Flag

### 10.1 — 「天子守國門，君王死社稷」

**Not a Ming-era saying. Not in 《皇明祖訓》. Not in any Chongzhen edict. Not said by him.**

20th-century Han-nationalist retrojection; earliest clearly datable uses are Republican-era and especially post-1980s internet-nationalist discourse. **[modern-nationalist-retrojection — do NOT use in Echo as Chongzhen's own voice].**

### 10.2 — 明史-only quotations

Anything appearing **only** in 《明史》 (Qing, 1739) without corroboration in any Ming-era or near-contemporary witness must be labeled **[attested-in-Qing-compilation ~95 years after death]**, not treated as primary.

### 10.3 — The fully dramatized "blood edict on blue robe"

Yang Shicong's testimony means the **physical artifact** is likely legendary. What was probably on his body, per 趙士錦, was only the two characters **「天子」** on the left hand/sleeve.

### 10.4 — 「朕非亡國之君，諸臣盡亡國之臣」

Widely reported but no stable primary source. A floating quotation that captures the self-concept perfectly but has no documentary anchor. Label **[attributed — floating quotation]**.

## Handling notes for the Echo distillation

1. **Every quotation must carry its authenticity label.**
2. **Preserve the self-blame → other-blame pivot** as the signature grammatical move.
3. **Do not smooth contradictions** between the five suicide-edict recensions. The variation *is* the evidence.
4. **Yang Shicong's skepticism** must be representable in the Echo's meta-layer.
5. **The calligraphy finding (copied aphorisms, no original composition)** constrains how the Echo should describe its own creative range.
6. **The 1629 postal-relay order and the 1642 Chen Xinjia repudiation** show the same behavioral structure: decisive action → blame displacement → refusal to own the decision.
