# Freud Echo — Research Dimension 1: Primary Works

**Scope:** Freud's own surviving authored corpus (1895–1940), its critical editions, signature vocabulary, intellectual antecedents as Freud cites them, and the major doctrinal revisions across forty-five years of writing. Labels: [documented] = demonstrable in the German text / SE / GW; [attested] = reported by contemporaries or close collaborators; [attributed] = ascribed to Freud in secondary literature without a primary citation reproduced here; [modern-scholarly-inference] = later historiographic reconstruction; [legendary] = hagiographic or unsubstantiated.

---

## 1. The Critical Editions

### 1.1 The *Standard Edition of the Complete Psychological Works of Sigmund Freud* (SE)
- **24 volumes, Hogarth Press & Institute of Psycho-Analysis, London, 1953–1974.** General editor: **James Strachey**, in collaboration with Anna Freud, assisted by Alix Strachey and Alan Tyson; indexing by Angela Richards. [documented; web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Standard_Edition_of_the_Complete_Psychological_Works_of_Sigmund_Freud]
- Volume breakdown (abridged, canonical): I = Pre-psychoanalytic publications and unpublished drafts (incl. *Project for a Scientific Psychology*); II = *Studies on Hysteria*; IV–V = *Die Traumdeutung*; VI = *Psychopathology of Everyday Life*; VII = Dora + *Three Essays*; VIII = *Jokes*; X = Little Hans + Rat Man; XII = Schreber + papers on technique; XIII = *Totem and Taboo*; XIV = History of psychoanalytic movement + metapsychology papers + *On Narcissism* + *Mourning and Melancholia*; XV–XVI = *Introductory Lectures*; XVII = Wolf Man ("From the History of an Infantile Neurosis") + "The 'Uncanny'"; XVIII = *Beyond the Pleasure Principle* + *Group Psychology*; XIX = *The Ego and the Id*; XX = *Inhibitions, Symptoms and Anxiety* + *Autobiographical Study* + *Question of Lay Analysis*; XXI = *Future of an Illusion* + *Civilization and Its Discontents*; XXII = *New Introductory Lectures*; XXIII = *Moses and Monotheism* + *Outline of Psycho-Analysis* + "Analysis Terminable and Interminable"; XXIV = Indexes. [documented; web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Standard_Edition_of_the_Complete_Psychological_Works_of_Sigmund_Freud]

### 1.2 *Gesammelte Werke* (GW)
- **18 volumes, S. Fischer Verlag, Frankfurt, 1940–1952** (with a supplementary *Nachtragsband* 1987). The German reference edition. [documented]

### 1.3 New Penguin Freud (Penguin Modern Classics, 2002– )
- **General editor Adam Phillips.** Each volume translated by a different literary translator; Phillips explicitly refused uniform psychoanalytic terminology. Organised thematically, not chronologically. Sander Gilman (1991) and Phillips both argue Strachey's consistency is the problem: it converted a stylist into a scientist. [documented; web: https://www.lrb.co.uk/the-paper/v29/n19/adam-phillips/after-strachey] [web: https://robertboynton.com/adam-phillips-and-the-new-freud/]

### 1.4 The Strachey Controversy
- Bruno Bettelheim, *Freud and Man's Soul* (1982): SE medicalized Freud's German. The Seele (soul) became "mental apparatus"; das Es / das Ich / das Über-Ich (the It / the I / the Over-I) became Latinate Id / Ego / Super-Ego, "evok[ing] no emotion and keep[ing] the reader cold and detached." Strachey's stated "imaginary model" was "some English man of science of wide education born in the middle of the nineteenth century." [documented, with translation register disputed] [web: https://www.lrb.co.uk/the-paper/v29/n19/adam-phillips/after-strachey]
- Register difference is load-bearing: Phillips-era translators will render *Trieb* as "drive" (Strachey: "instinct," which in English carries ethological baggage Freud did not intend). Strachey's "cathexis" (for *Besetzung*, everyday German for "occupation/investment") is the most cited single offence. [documented]

## 2. Major Works — Year, Language, Edition, Central Claim

All originally German unless noted.

### 2.1 *Studies on Hysteria* / *Studien über Hysterie* (1895, with Josef Breuer) — SE II
- Joint preliminary communication (1893) + Breuer's Anna O. (Bertha Pappenheim, treated 1880–82) + four Freud cases (Emmy von N., Lucy R., Katharina, Elisabeth von R.) + theoretical chapters. [documented; web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Studies_on_Hysteria]
- **Central claim:** hysterical symptoms are symbolic residues of affect-laden memories that could not be discharged; "the hysteric suffers mainly from reminiscences." The "talking cure" (Anna O.'s coinage) / cathartic method releases trapped affect through abreaction. [documented]

### 2.2 *Project for a Scientific Psychology* / *Entwurf einer Psychologie* (1895) — SE I
- **Status: unpublished in his lifetime.** Drafted September–October 1895; sent in two notebooks to Wilhelm Fliess; a third notebook (on repression) withheld. Rediscovered among the Fliess correspondence after Freud's death; first published 1950. Title supplied by editors. Freud himself called it "Psychology for Neurologists," using the neurone designations φ, ψ, ω. [documented; web: https://www.encyclopedia.com/psychology/dictionaries-thesauruses-pictures-and-press-releases/project-scientific-psychology]
- **Central claim:** a quantitative, neurone-based model of mental life in which Q (quantity of excitation) flows between φ (permeable perceptual), ψ (impermeable memory), and ω (conscious/quality) systems. Freud later disowned it as premature neurology; modern commentators (Pribram, Lothane) read it as the hydraulic template underlying all later metapsychology. [documented / modern-scholarly-inference]

### 2.3 *The Interpretation of Dreams* / *Die Traumdeutung* (1900) — SE IV–V
- Published November 1899, dated 1900; first edition 600 copies, took eight years to sell out. Eight editions in Freud's lifetime. [documented]
- **Central claim:** "The interpretation of dreams is the royal road to a knowledge of the unconscious activities of the mind" (SE V, p. 608). A dream is *die (verhüllte) Erfüllung eines (verdrängten) Wunsches* — "the (disguised) fulfilment of a (repressed) wish" (SE IV, p. 160). Distinction between *manifester Trauminhalt* / *latenter Trauminhalt* (manifest / latent content); the *Traumarbeit* (dream-work) operates by *Verdichtung* (condensation), *Verschiebung* (displacement), *Darstellbarkeit* (considerations of representability), and *sekundäre Bearbeitung* (secondary revision). [documented; web: https://www.freud.org.uk/schools/resources/the-interpretation-of-dreams/wish-fulfilment/]

### 2.4 *The Psychopathology of Everyday Life* / *Zur Psychopathologie des Alltagslebens* (1901) — SE VI
- **Central claim:** parapraxes (*Fehlleistungen* — slips of the tongue, pen, forgetting of names, bungled actions) are not accidents but overdetermined compromise-formations revealing unconscious intent. The "Freudian slip" as popular shorthand derives from this book. [documented]

### 2.5 *Jokes and Their Relation to the Unconscious* / *Der Witz und seine Beziehung zum Unbewussten* (1905) — SE VIII
- **Central claim:** joke-work parallels dream-work (condensation, displacement) but serves discharge of aggression or sexuality bypassing the censor via social complicity; mapped three persons — teller, butt, listener. [documented]

### 2.6 *Three Essays on the Theory of Sexuality* / *Drei Abhandlungen zur Sexualtheorie* (1905) — SE VII
- **Six German editions: 1905, 1910, 1915, 1920, 1922 (unchanged), 1924.** Text grew ~50% (80→120 pp.) across revisions. [documented; web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three_Essays_on_the_Theory_of_Sexuality]
- **Three essays:** (1) The Sexual Aberrations; (2) Infantile Sexuality; (3) The Transformations of Puberty.
- **Central claim:** sexuality is not coextensive with genitality or reproduction; humans are "polymorphously perverse" from infancy; adult sexuality emerges through oral/anal/phallic/latent/genital stages; *Libido* is a quantifiable drive-energy. [documented]
- **Crucial revision-history:** the **1905 first edition contains no Oedipus complex**, no castration complex, no penis envy; pregenital organisation and the sexual theories of children were **added in 1915**; libido theory section extended 1915; the term *libido* re-weighted after 1920's dual-drive theory. [documented]

### 2.7 The Five Case Studies
- **Dora** — "Fragment of an Analysis of a Case of Hysteria" (*Bruchstück einer Hysterie-Analyse*). Treated 1900, published 1905. SE VII. Female hysteria, transference-failure (Freud's own later admission). [documented]
- **Little Hans** — "Analysis of a Phobia in a Five-Year-Old Boy" (*Analyse der Phobie eines fünfjährigen Knaben*), 1909, SE X. Horse phobia as Oedipal/castration anxiety; analysis conducted by the boy's father under Freud's supervision. [documented]
- **Rat Man** — "Notes Upon a Case of Obsessional Neurosis" (*Bemerkungen über einen Fall von Zwangsneurose*), 1909, SE X. Obsessional neurosis; ambivalence; unconscious hostility to father. [documented]
- **Schreber** — "Psycho-Analytic Notes on an Autobiographical Account of a Case of Paranoia (Dementia Paranoides)," 1911, SE XII. Analysis of Daniel Paul Schreber's *Denkwürdigkeiten eines Nervenkranken* (1903) as a case of paranoid repression of homosexual desire — Freud never met the patient. [documented]
- **Wolf Man** — "From the History of an Infantile Neurosis" (*Aus der Geschichte einer infantilen Neurose*), written 1914, published 1918, SE XVII. Sergei Pankejeff. Primal scene (*Urszene*), Nachträglichkeit, infantile neurosis reconstructed retroactively. [documented]

### 2.8 *Totem and Taboo* / *Totem und Tabu* (1913) — SE XIII
- Four essays originally serialised in *Imago* 1912–13. Subtitled "Einige Übereinstimmungen im Seelenleben der Wilden und der Neurotiker" — "Some Points of Agreement between the Mental Lives of Savages and Neurotics." [documented; web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Totem_and_Taboo]
- **Central claim:** applies Oedipus to phylogeny via Darwin's primal-horde hypothesis + Robertson Smith's totem-sacrifice theory: the sons band together, kill and eat the father, feel guilt and ambivalence, erect the totem and the incest taboo. Religion is collective deferred guilt. "In the beginning was the Deed." [documented]

### 2.9 *On Narcissism: An Introduction* / *Zur Einführung des Narzißmus* (1914) — SE XIV
- **Central claim:** libido is not only invested in objects (*Objektlibido*) but also in the ego (*Ichlibido*); primary narcissism is the original infantile state. Introduces the *Ich-Ideal* (ego ideal), precursor to the 1923 superego. Destabilises the earlier ego-drives-vs-sexual-drives dualism — a major theoretical inflection point. [documented; web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/On_Narcissism]

### 2.10 Metapsychological Papers (1915) — SE XIV
- Written over a few weeks in 1915 as a projected twelve-paper book; only five published in his lifetime, seven destroyed. Extant: "Triebe und Triebschicksale" (*Instincts and Their Vicissitudes*), "Die Verdrängung" (*Repression*), "Das Unbewusste" (*The Unconscious*), "Metapsychologische Ergänzung zur Traumlehre" (*A Metapsychological Supplement to the Theory of Dreams*), "Trauer und Melancholie" (*Mourning and Melancholia*, published 1917). [documented]
- **Central claim:** any mental process can be described along three registers — *dynamisch* (forces/conflict), *topisch* (locus in Ucs/Pcs/Cs), *ökonomisch* (quantity of cathexis). This triad defines "metapsychology." [documented; web: https://pep-web.org/browse/document/SE.014.0159A.P193]

### 2.11 *Mourning and Melancholia* / *Trauer und Melancholie* (1917 [1915]) — SE XIV
- **Central claim:** in mourning, the lost object is gradually decathected; in melancholia, the lost object is introjected — "the shadow of the object fell upon the ego" (*Der Schatten des Objekts fiel... auf das Ich*, SE XIV, p. 249) — and the ego is then punished by an agency that will later be named the superego. First theorisation of identification as the mechanism of ego-formation. [documented; web: https://www.jungiananalysts.org.uk/.../Freud-S.-1917-Mourning-and-Melancholia]

### 2.12 *Beyond the Pleasure Principle* / *Jenseits des Lustprinzips* (1920) — SE XVIII
- **Central claim:** traumatic-dream repetition, children's fort/da play, transference-repetition, and the biological tendency to restore an earlier state all exceed the pleasure principle and compel the hypothesis of a *Todestrieb* (death drive) in primary dualism with *Lebenstriebe* / *Eros*. The organism "wishes to die only in its own fashion." [documented; web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beyond_the_Pleasure_Principle]
- The Greek mytheme **Thanatos is not Freud's term** — introduced by Wilhelm Stekel and popularised among Freud's followers. Freud wrote *Todestrieb*. [documented; web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Death_drive]

### 2.13 *The Ego and the Id* / *Das Ich und das Es* (1923) — SE XIX
- **Central claim:** the first topography (Ucs / Pcs / Cs) cannot accommodate the discovery that *defences are themselves unconscious*. Replaced by the **structural model**: *das Es* (id) — reservoir of drive, pleasure-principle-governed; *das Ich* (ego) — reality-testing, bound, emergent from Es through contact with external world; *das Über-Ich* (super-ego) — heir of the Oedipus complex, vehicle of the ego ideal, source of guilt. "Wo Es war, soll Ich werden" (*Where It was, there I shall become*, later in *New Introductory Lectures*, Lecture XXXI). [documented; web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Ego_and_the_Id]
- *Es* borrowed from Georg Groddeck. [documented]

### 2.14 *Inhibitions, Symptoms and Anxiety* / *Hemmung, Symptom und Angst* (1926) — SE XX
- **Central claim:** reverses the 1895–1917 view (repression causes anxiety by damming libido). Now: **anxiety is the signal that triggers repression**, a response of the ego to a danger-situation (loss of object, castration, loss of superego love). Distinguishes *Realangst* (realistic anxiety) from *neurotische Angst*. [documented]

### 2.15 *The Future of an Illusion* / *Die Zukunft einer Illusion* (1927) — SE XXI
- **Central claim:** religion is a *neurosis of humanity* — a collective wish-fulfilment (protection by a cosmic Father) built on infantile helplessness. An "illusion" is not necessarily a delusion; it is a belief motivated by a wish. Science (*Logos*) is the slow, modest alternative. [documented]

### 2.16 *Civilization and Its Discontents* / *Das Unbehagen in der Kultur* (1930) — SE XXI
- Title's *Unbehagen* = malaise/uneasiness/discontent — no exact English equivalent. [documented; web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Civilization_and_Its_Discontents]
- **Central claim:** civilisation is purchased by increasing renunciation of aggressive and libidinal drive; the death drive is turned inward, introjected as superego, experienced as guilt. "The price we pay for our advance in civilization is a loss of happiness through the heightening of the sense of guilt" (SE XXI, p. 134). Opens with the "oceanic feeling" dialogue with Romain Rolland. [documented]

### 2.17 *New Introductory Lectures on Psychoanalysis* / *Neue Folge der Vorlesungen zur Einführung in die Psychoanalyse* (1933) — SE XXII
- Never delivered as actual lectures (by 1933 Freud's jaw cancer prevented lecturing). A sequel to the 1916–17 *Introductory Lectures*. Contains the canonical formulation of the structural model (Lecture XXXI, "Dissection of the Psychical Personality"), the mature theory of anxiety, the much-contested lecture on femininity ("Über die Weiblichkeit," Lecture XXXIII), and the programmatic "*Wo Es war, soll Ich werden*." [documented]

### 2.18 *Moses and Monotheism* / *Der Mann Moses und die monotheistische Religion* (1939) — SE XXIII
- Final book, published in London after emigration. Three essays. **Central claim:** Moses was an Egyptian; Mosaic monotheism derives from Akhenaten's Aton cult; the Jews murdered Moses; the crime and its deferred return structure Jewish monotheism and give it its peculiar continuity. Phylogenetic return of the repressed applied to a single people. [documented]

### 2.19 *An Outline of Psycho-Analysis* / *Abriss der Psychoanalyse* (1940, posthumous) — SE XXIII
- Unfinished; begun in London after emigration 1938; work halted by cancer surgery. Published 1940. Freud intended "to bring together the tenets of psycho-analysis and to state them, as it were, dogmatically — in the most concise form and in the most unequivocal terms." Closest thing to a Freudian catechism in his own hand. [documented; web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/An_Outline_of_Psychoanalysis]

## 3. Core Arguments Recurring ≥3 Times Across the Corpus

1. **The unconscious (*das Unbewusste*) is the core of mental life.** Recurs: *Traumdeutung* ch. VII (1900), "Note on the Unconscious" (1912), "Das Unbewusste" (1915), *Ego and the Id* (1923), *Outline* (1940). "The unconscious is the true psychical reality" (SE V, p. 613). [documented]
2. **Repression (*Verdrängung*) as the cornerstone mechanism.** Freud: "The theory of repression is the corner-stone on which the whole structure of psycho-analysis rests" ("On the History of the Psycho-Analytic Movement," 1914, SE XIV, p. 16). Recurs throughout. [documented]
3. **Oedipus complex.** Arises *Traumdeutung* (1900, without the name), named 1910, universalised in *Three Essays* from 1915, mythologised in *Totem and Taboo* (1913), structuralised as origin of the superego in *Ego and Id* (1923). [documented]
4. **Overdetermination (*Überdeterminierung*).** Symptoms, dreams, parapraxes, jokes are never caused by one motive; always a compromise-formation of multiple unconscious currents. *Studies on Hysteria*, *Traumdeutung*, *Psychopathology*, *Jokes*. [documented]
5. **The two topographies (first Ucs/Pcs/Cs; second Es/Ich/Über-Ich).** Maintained in uneasy coexistence after 1923; Freud never fully abandoned the first. [documented]
6. **Transference (*Übertragung*).** From the "Postscript" to Dora (1905) through *Introductory Lectures* XXVII–XXVIII (1916–17) through *Outline* (1940). Initially a "resistance," revalued as the essential analytic instrument. [documented; web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transference]
7. **Free association (*freie Assoziation*) as the fundamental rule.** Displaces hypnosis from mid-1890s onward. [documented]
8. **Primary and secondary process.** Primary: unbound energy, condensation/displacement, timelessness, no contradiction, governed by pleasure principle (the Ucs). Secondary: bound, logical, time-and-contradiction-honouring, governed by reality principle (the Pcs/Cs). Formalised *Traumdeutung* ch. VII. [documented]
9. **Pleasure principle vs. reality principle.** "Two Principles of Mental Functioning" (1911), *Beyond the Pleasure Principle* (1920), *Outline*. [documented]
10. **Eros vs. Todestrieb (after 1920).** Dualistic drive theory replacing ego-vs-sexual drives. [documented]
11. **Infantile sexuality and psychosexual stages** (oral / anal / phallic / latency / genital). *Three Essays* + later revisions. [documented]
12. **Return of the repressed (*Wiederkehr des Verdrängten*).** In symptoms, dreams, slips, religion, culture. *Moses* generalises this to a people. [documented]

## 5. Intellectual Antecedents as Freud Cites Them

- **Ernst Brücke** (physiologist, Vienna) — Freud's doctoral supervisor; School of Helmholtz reductionism; "Brücke's great authority" (*Autobiographical Study*, SE XX). The hydraulic, energetic model of the *Project* is Brückean. [documented; web: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/0964704X.2022.2074280]
- **Theodor Meynert** (neuroanatomist, Vienna) — early mentor; cerebral-localisation model; later breach over hypnosis. [documented]
- **Jean-Martin Charcot** (Salpêtrière, Paris) — "*C'est la théorie, mais ça n'empêche pas d'exister*" ("It's the theory, but that doesn't stop it from existing") — Charcot's retort cited by Freud as formative: clinical fact over theoretical tidiness. Freud translated Charcot's *Leçons* into German (1886) and named his eldest son Jean-Martin. [documented]
- **Josef Breuer** — collaborator, senior figure, patron. Provided Anna O. and the cathartic method. Splits over sexual aetiology by ~1896. [documented]
- **Wilhelm Fliess** (Berlin, ENT, numerology) — Freud's epistolary intimate 1887–1902; the *Project* and most of Freud's self-analysis are documented only in these letters. A quasi-primary source: Marie Bonaparte bought and preserved them against Freud's wishes. [documented]
- **Schopenhauer & Nietzsche** — ambivalent. Freud *denied* having read Nietzsche until late; acknowledged Schopenhauer's Will as proto-drive. Parallels (unconscious, repression, drive, sublimation) are close enough that modern scholarship (Gardner, Ellenberger, Gupta) treats the denial itself as symptomatic. [documented denial / modern-scholarly-inference on actual debt; web: https://scielo.org.za/scielo.php?script=sci_arttext&pid=S1445-73772015000200002]
- **Helmholtz, Fechner** — energy-conservation and the constancy principle; "the principle of constancy" in *Beyond the Pleasure Principle* is explicitly Fechnerian. [documented]
- **Darwin** — primal horde hypothesis reused in *Totem and Taboo*; phylogenetic inheritance assumption throughout. [documented]

## 6. Major Revisions (The Contradictions Between Earlier and Later Freud)

### 6.1 Seduction theory → fantasy (1897)
- 1896: "The Aetiology of Hysteria" (SE III) — every hysteria rests on actual infantile sexual seduction by an adult, usually the father.
- **21 September 1897, letter to Fliess:** "*Ich glaube an meine Neurotica nicht mehr*" — "I no longer believe in my neurotica." Reasons Freud gives: clinical failures; epidemiological implausibility ("in all cases the father, not excluding my own, had to be accused"); the unconscious cannot distinguish memory from fantasy. [documented; web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freud's_seduction_theory]
- Becomes infantile sexuality + Oedipal fantasy from 1905. Masson (1984) and others have argued Freud suppressed real abuse; mainstream scholarship disputes this.

### 6.2 First topography → structural model (1915 → 1923)
- Through 1915 *metapsychology*: Ucs / Pcs / Cs by criterion of accessibility to consciousness.
- Breakdown: defences themselves turn out to be unconscious — "a portion of the ego... is *Ucs* in the full sense" (*Ego and Id*, SE XIX, p. 18).
- *Ego and the Id* (1923): Es / Ich / Über-Ich, independent of conscious accessibility; quality (conscious vs unconscious) is now a *property* of contents and not identical with agency. [documented]

### 6.3 First drive theory → death drive (1905/1915 → 1920)
- Early dualism: ego-drives (self-preservation) vs sexual drives (libido). *On Narcissism* (1914) already destabilised this by showing the ego is libidinally cathected.
- *Beyond the Pleasure Principle* (1920): new dualism *Eros* (both ego-drives and sexual drives folded together) vs *Todestrieb*. Major internal dissent — Jones, even Anna Freud, uncomfortable; Freud held firm. [documented]

### 6.4 Anxiety reversed (1926)
- Through 1917: repression dams up libido, which is converted into anxiety.
- *Inhibitions, Symptoms and Anxiety* (1926): **anxiety causes repression** — a signal in the ego of a danger-situation. [documented]

### 6.5 Femininity — perpetually unfinished
- *Three Essays* treated the female as a deviation from the male case. "Some Psychical Consequences of the Anatomical Distinction between the Sexes" (1925) and *New Introductory Lectures* XXXIII (1933): the girl's pre-oedipal attachment to the mother is as decisive as the oedipal triangle, penis envy, phallic-phase analogy of clitoris to penis. Freud's own "What does a woman want?" and his admission that female sexuality is a "dark continent" (*The Question of Lay Analysis*, 1926, SE XX, p. 212) are documented. [documented]

## 7. Disputed / Abandoned / Quasi-Primary Works

- **The *Project for a Scientific Psychology* (1895):** Freud himself withdrew the manuscript; never cited it in his published work; the editors (Strachey, Anna Freud, Bonaparte) published it posthumously. Its status as "the *Urtext* of metapsychology" is a retrospective scholarly construction (Strachey's editor's introduction, SE I; Pribram & Gill 1976). [documented as abandoned / modern-scholarly-inference as Urtext]
- **The Fliess correspondence (1887–1902):** quasi-primary. Freud asked Fliess to destroy the letters; Fliess's widow sold them; Bonaparte bought them against Freud's explicit instructions and ensured preservation. First partial publication *Aus den Anfängen der Psychoanalyse* (1950); full Masson edition 1985. Indispensable for dating the *Project*, the self-analysis, the seduction-theory collapse, the first formulation of the Oedipus complex (letter of 15 October 1897). [documented]
- **The seven destroyed metapsychology papers (1915):** burned by Freud; titles partially known (consciousness, anxiety, conversion hysteria, obsessional neurosis, transference neuroses, the projection/paranoia paper, and one on sublimation). Lost to the corpus. [documented / attested]
- **An Outline of Psycho-Analysis (1940):** unfinished at his death. [documented]

## 8. Quoted Passages for Voice Distillation

- "*Wo Es war, soll Ich werden.*" — *New Introductory Lectures*, Lecture XXXI, SE XXII, p. 80. Strachey: "Where id was, there ego shall be." Literal: "Where It was, there I shall become." [documented]
- "*Die Traumdeutung... ist die Via regia zur Kenntnis des Unbewussten im Seelenleben.*" — *Traumdeutung*, SE V, p. 608. [documented]
