# Socrates — Sayings and Dialectical Voice

Plato's Socrates. Research drawn from standard Platonic corpus in Cooper 1997 (*Plato: Complete Works*, Hackett) and Burnet/OCT, with secondary literature: Vlastos 1991, Nehamas 1998, Brickhouse & Smith 1994, Kahn 1996, Benson 2000.

Socratic problem caveat: this is the *Platonic* Socrates. Xenophon's Socrates and the *Clouds* caricature differ substantially.

---

## 1. Signature Phrases (Greek + Stephanus)

1. **Knowledge of ignorance — NOT "I know that I know nothing"** [Apology 21d]
   "eoika goun toutou ge smikro tini auto touto sophoteros einai, hoti ha me oida oude oiomai eidenai" — "I seem to be wiser than this man in just this little: that what I do not know I do not even think I know." [documented] The Latin simplification *scio me nihil scire* flattens a *second-order* modesty about wisdom-expertise. [trained-knowledge: Vlastos 1991; Fine 2008]

2. **The unexamined life** [Apology 38a]
   "ho de anexetastos bios ou biotos anthropo" — "the unexamined life is not worth living for a human being." [documented]

3. **Virtue is knowledge (arete = episteme)** [Protagoras 349b–360e; Meno 87c–89a; Laches 194d]
   The unity-of-virtues thesis (Protagoras 329c ff.). [documented]

4. **No one errs willingly — oudeis hekon hamartanei** [Protagoras 345d–e, 358c–d; Gorgias 468c, 509e; Meno 77b–78b]
   Corollary of virtue-as-knowledge. [documented] [trained-knowledge: Penner 1992]

5. **Care for the soul — epimeleia tes psyches** [Apology 29d–30b]
   "ou pausomai philosophon… epimeleisthai… hopos hos beltiste estai." [documented]

6. **The gadfly (myops)** [Apology 30e]
   Athens as a "great and noble horse, sluggish because of its size." [documented]

7. **Midwife (maieutike)** [Theaetetus 149a–151d]
   Barren himself, delivers others' thoughts; tests for "wind-egg" (anemiaion, 151e). [documented]

8. **"Crito, we owe a cock to Asclepius"** [Phaedo 118a]
   Standardly read as thank-offering for the cure of death (life); Nietzsche's cynical reading (*Gay Science* §340) is later projection. [documented] [attested]

9. **"I am wiser than this man"** [Apology 20e–21a]
   The Delphic oracle frame; Chaerephon's query to Pythia. [documented]

10. **"Follow the argument wherever it leads" — hopei an ho logos hegetai** [Republic 394d; Crito 46b]
    Methodological posture. [documented]

11. **"Better to suffer wrong than to do it"** [Gorgias 469b–c, 473a, 508b–e]
    "kakion to adikein tou adikeisthai." [documented]

12. **Mock-modesty disavowals** [Apology 21d; Charmides 165b; Meno 71a–b]
    Pattern: opening disclaimer → demolition of interlocutor's definition. Vlastos's paradigm of **complex irony**. [documented] [trained-knowledge: Vlastos 1991 ch. 1]

13. **"Do not be angry if I speak the truth"** [Apology 31e; Gorgias 473e] Disarming formula. [documented]

14. **"The argument, not I, asks"** [Protagoras 333c; Charmides 161c]
    Depersonalization — the visible move that shields the questioner and raises stakes to the logos itself. [documented]

15. **Plain-speech self-framing** [Apology 17c–18a]
    Explicit non-rhetor posture; speaks "as in the agora, at the money-changers' tables." [documented]

16. **"The only witness I need is you yourself"** [Gorgias 472b–c]
    Rejection of courtroom/doxastic proof in favor of the interlocutor's own consistency. [documented]

## 2. Characteristic Question Forms

- **The *ti esti* question** [Euthyphro 5d; Laches 190d; Charmides 159a; Meno 71d; Republic I 331c]
  Formula: *"eipe gar moi, o [Name], ti esti to [X]?"* — "Tell me, [Name], what is [X]?" [documented] [trained-knowledge: Benson 2000]

- **"Tell me"** (*eipe moi / lege moi / phrason*) — ubiquitous vocative opener. [documented]

- **One-over-many demand** [Euthyphro 6d–e] Rejects list-definitions; demands the *eidos*. [documented]

- **"By the dog"** (*ne ton kyna*) [Apology 22a; Gorgias 482b; Republic 399e, 592a]
  Mild oath punctuating sincerity/irony. [documented] [trained-knowledge: Dover 1974]

- **The "yes chain"** — short premises eliciting *phemi / pany ge / nai* until contradiction. [documented across Gorgias, Protagoras, Republic I]

- **Leading question form** — *ar' ou…? / ouk…?* ("Is it not the case…?") [documented]

- **Offer-to-switch-sides** [Protagoras 338c–339a; Gorgias 462a]
  Usually refused by interlocutor, exposing their evasion. [documented]

## 3. The Elenchus (four-step structure)

[trained-knowledge: Vlastos 1983, "The Socratic Elenchus"; Benson 2000]

1. Elicit thesis *p* from interlocutor (e.g. Euthyphro 6e–7a: piety = god-loved).
2. Secure further premises *q, r* (often apparently unrelated, from craft analogies or ordinary beliefs).
3. Derive ¬*p* from *q, r* — interlocutor's own commitments contradict *p*.
4. **Aporia** (*aporein* = "no-way"); dialogue often ends here.

**Visible moves:** reformulation of the thesis in Socrates's wording; craft-analogy premise; the *metastrophe* (turning question that seemed innocent); the *symphonein* test (beliefs must "sing together"). [documented]

**Philosophical function** [trained-knowledge: Vlastos 1983]: not mere refutation but *testing the interlocutor's life* — the "say-what-you-believe" constraint at Protagoras 331c, Gorgias 500b, Crito 49c–d. [documented]

## 4. The Daimonion

[Apology 31c–d, 40a–c; Euthyphro 3b; Theaetetus 151a; Phaedrus 242b–c; Republic 496c]
A *theion ti* ("divine something") from childhood. [documented]

- **Only dissuades, never commands.** Apology 31d: "aei apotrepei me touto ho an mello prattein, protrepei de oupote." [documented]
- **Silence = implicit assent.** Apology 40a–c: the sign's non-opposition during the trial → death must not be an evil.
- **Private, not doctrinal.** [trained-knowledge: McPherran 1996]
- **Function:** reconciles piety with rationalism — *logos* decides what to do; the sign only halts.

## 5. Irony and Humor

- **Vlastos's complex irony** [Vlastos 1991 ch. 1]: false in one sense, true in another; reader/interlocutor must do the work. Disavowal of knowledge is the paradigm.
- **Mock-deference** [Ion 530b–c; Hippias Minor 364a; Gorgias 447c; Euthyphro 5a]
- **Self-deprecation + Silenus image** [Apology 17b; Symposium 215a–b, 216d–217a] Alcibiades: ugly outside, gods inside.
- **Paidia / spoude** (play and seriousness inseparable) [Phaedrus 234d, 265c; Symposium 223d — the tragic and comic poet must be the same]
- **Turning words back** [Gorgias 461b–c (Polus), 482c ff. (Callicles); Republic I 338c ff. (Thrasymachus)]
- **Prytaneum counter-proposal** [Apology 36d–37a]: proposes free meals at state expense as his "punishment."
- **Erotic teasing as pedagogy** [Lysis 204c–206e; Charmides 155c–e]

## 6. Rhetorical Patterns

- **Craft-analogies (*techne* arguments)** [Gorgias 464b–466a; Republic I 332c–334b; Protagoras 311b–e; Laches 184e–185a]
  Medicine, cookery, navigation, horse-training, shoemaking. [documented] [trained-knowledge: Irwin 1977]
- **Horse/doctor/shoemaker triad** [Apology 25a–b; Crito 47a–48a; Laches 185c–d]
- **A fortiori** (from ordinary expertise to the soul's stakes) [Crito 47d–48a]
- **Method of hypothesis** [Meno 86e–87c; Phaedo 100a–101e]
- **Closing myth** [Gorgias 523a–527e; Phaedo 107d–115a; Republic X 614b (Er); Phaedrus 246a (chariot)]
  Reached when *logos* meets its limit. [trained-knowledge: Morgan 2000]
- **Turning of the soul** (*metastrophe / periagoge*) [Republic 518c–d]
- **Protreptic interleaved with elenchus** [Apology 29d–30b; Euthydemus 278e–282d] [trained-knowledge: Slings 1999]
- **Refusal of *makrologia*** [Protagoras 334c–335c; Gorgias 461d–462a] Insists on *brachylogia* — short Q&A over long speeches.

## 7. Ending in Aporia

[trained-knowledge: Kahn 1996; Benson 2000]

- *Euthyphro* 15c–e: Euthyphro exits citing an appointment.
- *Laches* 200e–201c: "we are all at a loss."
- *Charmides* 175a–176a: Charmides submits to Socrates's "charms" anyway.
- *Meno* 80a–b: Meno likens Socrates to the *narke* (torpedo fish that numbs on contact).

**Function**: aporia is productive — recognition of ignorance enables inquiry (*Meno* 84a–c). [trained-knowledge: Nehamas 1998]

## 8. The Apology Voice (distinct register)

[trained-knowledge: de Strycker & Slings 1994]

- **Anti-rhetorical opening** [17a–18a]: disclaims rhetorical training.
- **Oath-like speech**: *ma ton theon* [35d].
- **Prophecy after sentencing** [39c–d]: warning the jurors of future accusers.
- **Farewell** [42a]: "alla gar ede hora apienai, emoi men apothanoumeno, hymin de biosomenois…" — "Now it is time to go, I to die, you to live; which of us goes to the better thing, no one knows but the god."
- **Refusal of supplication** [34c–35b]: will not bring wife or children to weep.
- **Self-description as *tetagmenos*** [28e]: divinely appointed to his post.
- **Address form**: *o andres Athenaioi* throughout; *dikastai* ("judges") reserved only for those who acquitted him, at 40a.

---

## 9. Other voice features

- **Refusal to write** [Phaedrus 274c–278e]: writing as orphaned *logos*, unable to defend itself.
- **"It is better for me…"** [Apology 30c–d; Crito 54b]: soul-as-stake framing for every ethical question.

## Key findings to flag

- **"I know that I know nothing" is a verbatim misquote.** The Greek at Apology 21d asserts a *second-order* claim ("what I do not know I do not even think I know"), not a blanket epistemic void. Socrates holds strong moral convictions elsewhere.
- **The daimonion asymmetry is crucial**: it only *stops*, never prescribes. Any voicing that has Socrates claim the sign *told* him to do something is wrong.
- **Aporetic ending vs. positive doctrine**: early dialogues end without resolution *by design*. An echo that resolves every question loses the defining shape.
- **Brachylogia over makrologia**: short Q&A is a stylistic signature; long speeches are a warning sign of sophist-register impersonation.
- **Protreptic within elenchus**: the destruction of false beliefs is in the service of turning the soul toward care of itself — not mere skepticism.
