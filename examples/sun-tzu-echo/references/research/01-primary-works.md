# Sun Wu (孙武) — Primary Works Research Findings

## 1. The Thirteen Chapters (十三篇)

The received *Sunzi Bingfa* has thirteen chapters (*shisan pian* 十三篇). Structure attested in Sima Qian's *Shiji* ch. 65 [attested] and confirmed by the Yinqueshan bamboo slips (c. 140–118 BCE) [documented]. Pre-Han, stable since at least mid-Warring States.

### Core argument per chapter

**Ch. 1 始計**: [documented] Opens 兵者，國之大事…不可不察也. Five Factors (五事): 道/天/地/將/法. Seven Comparisons (七計). Ends 兵者，詭道也 + twelve deception maneuvers + the **先勝** doctrine: 夫未戰而廟算勝者，得算多也 — temple calculation determines victory before mobilization.

**Ch. 2 作戰**: [documented] Economics of protracted war; 1,000 chariots, 100,000 troops, 1,000 ounces of silver per day. 兵貴勝，不貴久. 兵聞拙速，未睹巧之久. Feed off the enemy: 食敵一鐘，當吾二十鐘.

**Ch. 3 謀攻**: [documented] Doctrine of 全 (quán, wholeness): 上兵伐謀，其次伐交，其次伐兵，其下攻城. 不戰而屈人之兵，善之善者也. 知彼知己，百戰不殆. Five Victories (五勝).

**Ch. 4 軍形**: [documented] 先為不可勝，以待敵之可勝. 不可勝在己，可勝在敵. 勝兵先勝而後求戰 — the winning army wins first, then fights.

**Ch. 5 兵勢**: [documented] 奇/正 dialectic: 以正合，以奇勝. 奇正相生如循環之無端. Water sweeping stones. Falcon timing. 勢如彍弩，節如發機. 善戰者求之於勢，不責於人.

**Ch. 6 虛實**: [documented] 致人而不致於人. 攻其無備，出其不意. 形人而我無形. Concentrate 1:10. 兵形象水. 兵無常勢，水無常形.

**Ch. 7 軍爭**: [documented] 以迂為直，以患為利. 兵以詐立，以利動，以分合為變. Fūrinkazan: 其疾如風，其徐如林，侵掠如火，不動如山.

**Ch. 8 九變**: [documented] 途有所不由，軍有所不擊，城有所不攻，地有所不爭，君命有所不受. Five Dangers (五危) of a general.

**Ch. 9 行軍**: [documented] Four terrains to traverse. 30+ enemy signs. 令之以文，齊之以武.

**Ch. 10 地形**: [documented] Six terrain types, six general-caused calamities: 非天之災，將之過也. 知彼知己，勝乃不殆；知天知地，勝乃可全.

**Ch. 11 九地**: [documented] Longest chapter. Nine grounds. Death ground: 投之亡地然後存，陷之死地然後生. **Shuairan 率然** serpent of Mt. Chang.

**Ch. 12 火攻**: [documented] Five fire targets. Moral coda: 主不可以怒而興師，將不可以慍而致戰. 亡國不可以復存，死者不可以復生.

**Ch. 13 用間**: [documented] 先知者必取於人 (not spirits, not analogy, not calculation). Five spies: 鄉間/內間/反間/死間/生間. Cites Yi Yin (Shang) and Lü Ya/Taigong (Zhou) as former spies.

## 2. Authorship Status

**Traditional** (Sima Qian, *Shiji* 65): [attributed] Sun Wu native of Qi, served King Helü of Wu (r. 514–496 BCE); concubine-training anecdote; helped capture Chu capital Ying in 506 BCE.

**Song skepticism**: [attested] Ye Shi 葉適 (1150–1223) — the *Zuo Zhuan* covers Wu–Chu wars in detail and names Wu Zixu and Bo Pi, but never mentions Sun Wu. Extended by Chen Zhensun and Qing-era Yao Jiheng.

**Liang Qichao hypothesis**: Sun Bin composed the received text in 4th c. BCE. **No longer tenable** after Yinqueshan recovered both texts as separate works.

**Modern positions (preserved as contradictions, not harmonized)**:

| Question | Mair 2007 | Ames 1993 | Sawyer 1993 | Li Ling 2006 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sun Wu existed? | No | Probably, kernel figure | Probably | Yes, some form |
| Date | 350–275 BCE | 5th–4th c., layered | Late 5th / early 4th c. | Late Sp-Autumn kernel, Warring States edit |
| Daoism relation | Shaped by it | Parallel tributary | Independent | Parallel |
| Authorship unity | Multi-author | Sun school, coherent | Core + school | Core + school |
| 13-chapter originality | Late stabilization | Pre-Han | Pre-Han | Pre-Qin |

**Sun Bin overlap**: Sun Bin (c. 380–316 BCE), Qi strategist. *Sun Bin Bingfa* lost ~1,400 years, recovered at Yinqueshan in 16 chapters. Together Sunzi + Sun Bin establish a "Sun school" tradition.

## 3. Yinqueshan Bamboo Slips 銀雀山漢簡 (1972)

**Discovery**: April 10, 1972, Yinqueshan ("Silver Sparrow Mountain") southeast of Linyi, Shandong. Tomb 1 yielded ~4,942 bamboo strips in early-Han clerical script.

**Contents**: Sunzi Bingfa (13 chapters + extras), Sun Bin Bingfa (16 chapters — first recovery), Liu Tao, Wei Liaozi, Yanzi, divination texts.

**Additional Sunzi fragments at Yinqueshan** [documented]:
- **吳問 Wuwen** ("Questions of Wu") — dialogue on Jin's six noble houses falling in order. Prediction matches historical Partition of Jin (453/403 BCE) — evidence of Warring States compositional layer.
- **四變 Sibian** — exegesis on Ch. 8 "roads not to follow" passage
- **黃帝伐赤帝** — mythic framing
- **地形二 Dixing er** — Ch. 10 supplement
- **見吳王 Jian Wuwang** — older version of concubine anecdote, predating Sima Qian

**What Yinqueshan changed**: confirmed pre-Han 13-chapter structure; killed Liang Qichao hypothesis; established "Sun school"; but Wuwen proves received text contains mid-Warring States material.

## 4. Standard Critical Editions

**Chinese**:
- 《十一家注孫子》 *Shiyi jia zhu Sunzi* — Song compilation with Cao Cao (c. 200 CE, first commentator), Du Mu, Mei Yaochen, Zhang Yu, others. Textus receptus.
- Yang Bing'an 楊丙安, 《十一家注孫子校理》 (Zhonghua Shuju, 1999) — Yinqueshan-collated scholarly edition
- Li Ling 李零, 《孫子十三篇綜合研究》 (Zhonghua Shuju, 2006), 《孫子譯注》 (Zhonghua Shuju, 2007)

**English**:
- Giles 1910 (Luzac) — first scholarly English; pre-Yinqueshan
- Griffith 1963 (OUP) — Liddell Hart foreword; USMC general; pre-Yinqueshan
- Ames 1993 (Ballantine) — first English post-Yinqueshan
- Sawyer 1993 (Westview)
- Mair 2007 (Columbia UP)
- Minford 2002 (Penguin)
- Cleary 1988 (Shambhala) — popular, Buddhist/Daoist-inflected

**Canonical context**: 1080 CE, Emperor Shenzong of Song ordered *Wu jing qi shu* 武經七書 (Seven Military Classics). Order: Taigong *Liu Tao* → *Sima Fa* → *Sunzi* → Wu Qi *Wuzi* → *Wei Liaozi* → *Huang Shigong Sanlüe* → *Tang Taizong Li Weigong Wendui*.

## 5. Core Doctrinal Concepts (≥3× = true doctrine)

- **知 zhī (knowing)** [documented, 60+ occurrences] — Ch. 1, 3, 10, 13. Epistemic precondition of all strategy.
- **勢 shì (strategic configuration/momentum)** [documented] — Ch. 5 title; also Ch. 1, 4, 6, 11. 善戰者求之於勢不責於人. 如轉圓石於千仞之山. Translation disputes: Ames "strategic advantage," Mair untransliterated, Sawyer "strategic military power." Monograph: François Jullien, *The Propensity of Things* (Zone Books, 1995).
- **形 xíng (form/disposition)** [documented] — Ch. 4 title; paired with shi. Goal: 無形 (formlessness).
- **虛實 xū shí (empty/solid)** [documented] — Ch. 6 title; 避實而擊虛.
- **奇正 qí zhèng (unorthodox/orthodox)** [documented] — Ch. 5. 以正合，以奇勝. 奇正相生如循環之無端.
- **全 quán (wholeness/intact)** [documented] — Ch. 3. 全國為上，破國次之. 不戰而屈人之兵.
- **詭道 / 詐 guǐdào/zhà (deception)** [documented] — Ch. 1 programmatic; Ch. 7 reiterated. 兵者詭道也. 兵以詐立. Against Confucian moralism and *Sima Fa* ritual combat.
- **計 jì (calculation)** [documented] — Ch. 1 title. Temple calculation 廟算.
- **先勝 xiān shèng (victory before battle)** [documented] — Ch. 4. 勝兵先勝而後求戰.

Lesser-but-recurrent: 利 lì (advantage), 變 biàn (adaptation, Ch. 8), 節 jié (timing/trigger, Ch. 5), 將 jiàng (general — five virtues 智信仁勇嚴, five dangers 五危), 道 dào (ruler-people alignment, not metaphysical).

## 6. Intellectual Antecedents

Unlike *Mozi/Mencius*, Sunzi cites no thinkers by name.

- **Taigong 太公 (Jiang Ziya/Lü Shang)** — Zhou-founding strategist; *Liu Tao* attributed. Sunzi names "Lü Ya" only in Ch. 13 as archetypal spy/advisor — positioning himself in intelligence-statecraft lineage, not ritual-combat lineage.
- **Sima Fa 《司馬法》** — attributed to Sima Rangju of Qi (6th c. BCE). Ritual-ethical warfare. Sunzi breaks from this: deception, speed, economic calculation, abandonment of aristocratic combat ritual.
- **Proto-Daoist cosmology** — water-as-formlessness, xu/shi, qi/zheng, wu-wei-like 致人而不致於人 belong to the conceptual world of *Daodejing*. Mair (late dating): Sunzi *receives* Daoist cosmology. Ames (traditional dating): Sunzi is *independent tributary* to same stream.
- **Guanzi 管子 parallel** — quantified statecraft arithmetic of Guan Zhong of Qi. Sunzi's traditional home is Qi.

**Conspicuous non-citations**: no Confucius, Mencius, or Zhou classics; no Yellow Emperor cosmology; no theology or ancestor appeals.

## 7. Key Primary-Text Passages

- **Deception** (Ch. 1): 兵者，詭道也。故能而示之不能，用而示之不用，近而示之遠，遠而示之近。
- **Five Victories** (Ch. 3): 故知勝有五：知可以戰與不可以戰者勝；識眾寡之用者勝；上下同欲者勝；以虞待不虞者勝；將能而君不御者勝。
- **Formlessness** (Ch. 6): 故兵無常勢，水無常形；能因敵變化而取勝者，謂之神。
- **General's independence** (Ch. 8): 君命有所不受。
- **Cost of war** (Ch. 12): 亡國不可以復存，死者不可以復生。故明君慎之，良將警之。此安國全軍之道也。
- **Pre-battle calculation** (Ch. 1): 夫未戰而廟算勝者，得算多也... 多算勝，少算不勝，而況於無算乎。
- **Death ground** (Ch. 11): 投之亡地然後存，陷之死地然後生。
- **Knowing** (Ch. 10): 知彼知己，勝乃不殆；知天知地，勝乃可全。

## 8. Open Questions for Downstream Phases

1. Core engine: Ch. 1 (assessment), 3 (quan/planning), 4–6 (xing/shi/xushi triad), 13 (intelligence).
2. Reconciling deception-methodology (Ch. 1, 7) with ruler-caution/preservation ethics (Ch. 2, 12) — Sunzi is prudential-strategic, neither Machiavellian cynic nor Confucian ritualist.
3. Yinqueshan *Wuwen* — genuine Sun-school voice vs. Warring States gloss?
4. Translator choice: transliterate (*shi*, *qi/zheng*, *quan*) vs. translate. All four major moderns flag these as untranslatable; recommend Chinese terms with gloss.
5. Voice: individual Sun Wu vs. "Sun school" composite.
