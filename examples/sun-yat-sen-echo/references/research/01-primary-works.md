# Sun Yat-sen: Primary Works, Authorship, and Critical Editions

**Research Agent 1 / Primary Works** — foundation for a runnable cognitive echo of Sun Wen 孫文 / Sun Yixian 孫逸仙 / Sun Zhongshan 孫中山 (1866–1925). Every claim is label-tagged and source-origin tagged. Contradictions preserved, not harmonized.

## 1. Critical Editions

### 1.1 孫中山全集 Sun Zhongshan quanji — PRC 11-volume (Zhonghua Shuju 1981–1986)
- `[documented]` 11 volumes, compiled jointly by the Institute of Modern History of the Chinese Academy of Social Sciences, the Research Office on the History of the Republic of China within the Guangdong Provincial Academy of Social Sciences, and the Sun Yat-sen Research Room of Zhongshan University's Department of History. Published 1981–1986 by Zhonghua Shuju (中华书局). Principal PRC-side critical edition for three decades. `[web: https://www.amazon.com/Complete-Works-Yat-sen-1-16-Chinese/dp/7010147477]`
- `[documented]` A subsequent 16-volume expanded edition (ed. Shang Mingxuan 尚明轩) was published by People's Publishing House (人民出版社) in 2015, organized into 12 categories. `[web: https://www.amazon.com/Complete-Works-Yat-sen-1-16-Chinese/dp/7010147477]`

### 1.2 國父全集 Guofu quanji — ROC canon
- `[attested]` Published by the KMT Central Committee Party History Committee, Taipei; 1973 and 1974 volumes are the standard ROC scholarly reference. `[web: https://books.google.com/books/about/Sun_Yat_sen.html?id=vh7M1u4IGFkC]`
- `[modern-scholarly-inference]` Two competing canonical editions (PRC vs ROC) is itself a political fact; both Taipei and Beijing claim Sun as founding father — Bergère (Stanford 1998) opens on this. `[web: https://www.sup.org/books/title/?id=677]`

### 1.3 孫中山集外集 Sun Zhongshan ji wai ji — supplement
- `[documented]` Shanghai 1990, 10 categories (essays, speeches, conversations, letters, inscriptions, proclamations, manuscripts, published works), containing documents not in the main 孫中山全集. `[web: https://www.amazon.com/Sun-Zhongshan-wai-Mandarin-Chinese/dp/720800661X]`

### 1.4 Standard scholarly references
- `[documented]` Marie-Claire Bergère, *Sun Yat-sen*, trans. Janet Lloyd (Stanford University Press, 1998, 480 pp., ISBN 0-8047-3170-5). Reviewed in *China Quarterly* and *AHR* as "the most thorough book about Sun in a Western language." `[web: https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/china-quarterly/article/abs/sun-yatsen-by-marieclaire-bergere-translated-by-janet-lloyd-stanford-stanford-university-press-1998-vii-480-pp-4950-isbn-0804731705/A409EE0F9E09911E4180088C3AC900A4]`
- `[documented]` Paul M. A. Linebarger, *The Political Doctrines of Sun Yat-sen: An Exposition of the San Min Chu I* (Johns Hopkins Press, 1937). Bias caveat: Linebarger's father was legal advisor to the ROC government; Sun was godfather to the author. `[web: https://www.gutenberg.org/files/39356/39356-pdf.pdf]`

## 2. Corpus Inventory

Categories: **[A] clearly Sun's**, **[B] substantially Sun but edited/transcribed**, **[C] collaborative / ghost-written with attribution**, **[D] disputed**.

### 2.1 1894 — 上李鴻章書 Letter to Li Hongzhang `[A]`
- `[documented]` ~8,000-character petition carried to Tianjin; audience denied. Published two parts, Sept–Oct 1894, in 萬國公報 *Wanguo gongbao*. `[web: https://sites.asiasociety.org/chinawealthpower/suns-letter-to-li-hongzhang/]`
- `[documented]` Core formula (Asia Society translation): "making the best use of every talent, tapping the potential of every piece of land, making everything serve its purpose, and opening markets for its goods" (人能盡其才、地能盡其利、物能盡其用、貨能暢其流). Reformist, not revolutionary. `[web: https://sites.asiasociety.org/chinawealthpower/suns-letter-to-li-hongzhang/]`
- `[modern-scholarly-inference]` Sharpest dating-line in his intellectual biography: until this rejection (and the Yalu defeat) Sun was a Self-Strengthener; after it, revolutionary. Revive China Society founded Honolulu 24 November 1894. `[web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Revive_China_Society]`

### 2.2 1897 — *Kidnapped in London* `[C]` — collaborative English
- `[documented]` Published 1897 by J. W. Arrowsmith, Bristol. Full text at Internet Archive and Wikisource. `[web: https://archive.org/details/kidnappedinlondo00suny]` `[web: https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Kidnapped_in_London]`
- `[attested]` Collaborative: written with James Cantlie and Edwin Collins after Sun's release. Cantlie was Sun's former Hong Kong medical teacher; Collins had extensive editorial involvement. `[web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun_Yat-sen]`
- `[attested]` Patrick Anderson, *The Lost Book of Sun Yatsen and Edwin Collins* (Routledge 2017) argues Collins's share has been systematically understated. `[web: https://www.routledge.com/The-Lost-Book-of-Sun-Yatsen-and-Edwin-Collins/Anderson/p/book/9781138330153]`

### 2.3 1904 — *The True Solution of the Chinese Question* `[A]` — English pamphlet
- `[documented]` Autumn 1904, New York; 10,000 copies printed, financed by "Mr. E. Williams." Full text at Sunology database. `[web: https://sunology.yatsen.gov.tw/detail/7c1695aef9ee30e2eb989a0a488c06a8/]`
- `[documented]` Written during Russo-Japanese War; appeals to "the people of the civilized world, particularly the United States." States revolutionaries intend to model new government after America's. `[web: https://sunology.yatsen.gov.tw/detail/7c1695aef9ee30e2eb989a0a488c06a8/]`

### 2.4 1905 — Tongmenghui Manifesto and *Minbao* essays `[B]`
- `[documented]` 1905 Tongmenghui oath: 驅除韃虜，恢復中華，創立民國，平均地權 "Expel the Tatar barbarians, revive Zhonghua, establish a Republic, distribute land equally among the people." `[web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun_Yat-sen]`
- `[documented]` Three-stage framework prescribed in 1905 manifesto: military government → transitional period → fully constitutional democracy. `[web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tongmenghui]`
- `[attested]` 1905–1907: Sun, Wang Jingwei, Hu Hanmin, Song Jiaoren wrote articles in *Minbao* developing the Three Principles. `[web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three_Principles_of_the_People]`

### 2.5 1917 — 民權初步 Primer of Democracy `[A]` — Part 3 of 建國方略
- `[documented]` Originally titled 會議通則, completed February 1917; published April 1917 by Zhonghua Book Company, Shanghai. Later Part 3 (社會建設) of 建國方略. `[web: https://baike.baidu.com/en/item/The%20General%20Plan%20of%20National%20Construction/1455686]`
- `[modern-scholarly-inference]` Effectively an adaptation of Robert's *Rules of Order* for Chinese civic organizations; Sun's aim was pedagogical — teach citizens assembly mechanics before expecting democracy to function. `[web: https://www.gutenberg.org/cache/epub/39356/pg39356-images.html]`

### 2.6 1917 — *The Vital Problem of China* / 中國存亡問題 `[A]`
- `[attested]` Composed 1917 against the Chinese government's capitulation to British demand that China enter WWI against Germany. `[web: http://chinese.larouchepub.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/04/sun-yat-sen-the-vital-problem-of-china-eng.pdf]`

### 2.7 1918 — *Memoirs of a Chinese Revolutionary* `[A/B]`
- `[documented]` Published 1918. Covers life through 1912. Internet Archive full text. `[web: https://archive.org/details/memoirsofchinese0000suny]`
- `[attested]` First English-for-Western-readers statement of "Action is easy; knowledge is difficult" — prefiguring 孫文學說 (1919). `[web: https://china-journal.org/2016/06/04/sun-yat-sen-memoirs-of-a-chinese-revolutionary/]`

### 2.8 1919 — 孫文學說 Sun Wen's Doctrine / Psychological Reconstruction `[A]` — Part 1 of 建國方略
- `[documented]` Preface 30 December 1918; Volume 1 titled 知難行易 printed and published by Shanghai's 強華書局 on 20 May 1919. `[web: https://zh.wikipedia.org/zh-hant/%E5%AD%AB%E6%96%87%E5%AD%B8%E8%AA%AA]` `[web: https://sunology.yatsen.gov.tw/detail/ad2bb3bd1762b20c3b066486ba0077e0/]`
- `[documented]` Explicitly inverts the 尚書·說命 adage 知之非艱，行之惟艱. Sun: 行之非艱，知之惟艱 / 行易知難. Ten empirical examples (Ch 1: eating; Ch 2: use of money; etc.) showing people act without understanding. `[web: https://modernchina.org/article/6164.html]`
- `[modern-scholarly-inference]` Positioned against both Zhu Xi's 知先行後 and Wang Yangming's 知行合一; Sun's move is to **split** knowledge and action and call action cheap, thereby liberating the revolutionary from paralysis. `[web: https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/review-of-politics/article/abs/wang-yangming-and-the-ideology-of-sun-yatsen/02442FC360B77BE147F39D698270D385]`
- `[modern-scholarly-inference]` Contradictory reception — some commentators claim Sun admired Wang Yangming (Yangmingism wiki); the text breaks with 知行合一. Resolution: admired Wang's activist spirit, rejected his epistemology. `[web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yangmingism]`

### 2.9 1921 — *The International Development of China* / 實業計劃 `[A]` — Part 2 of 建國方略
- `[documented]` English original. Preface dated 25 April 1921; complete English edition by Commercial Press, Shanghai, 1920 (some sources 1921). Full text at Project Gutenberg. Chinese title 實業計劃. `[web: https://www.gutenberg.org/files/45188/45188-h/45188-h.htm]`
- `[documented]` Proposes large-scale foreign-capital participation in Chinese infrastructure (ports, railways, hydroelectric — includes an early proposal effectively for the Three Gorges Dam). `[web: https://www.gutenberg.org/files/45188/45188-h/45188-h.htm]`
- `[attested]` "Sun Yat-sen particularly treasured this English book, considering it as a general plan for China's peaceful development and even more important than the armed struggles he led." `[web: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/17535654.2021.2100641]`
- `[modern-scholarly-inference]` Single clearest case where Sun wrote **in English first**; the standard Chinese text in 孫中山全集 is translated from Sun's own English.

### 2.10 1923 — Sun-Joffe Manifesto `[C]`
- `[documented]` Issued Shanghai, 26 January 1923 with Adolf Joffe. `[web: https://www.britannica.com/event/Sun-Joffe-Manifesto]`
- `[documented]` Key clause: "Dr. Sun Yat-sen holds that the Communistic order or even the Soviet system cannot actually be introduced into China, because there do not exist here the conditions for the successful establishment of either Communism or Sovietism." Joffe "entirely concurred" and stated "China's paramount and most pressing problem is to achieve national unification and to attain full national independence." `[web: https://www.britannica.com/event/Sun-Joffe-Manifesto]`
- `[documented]` Also covers Outer Mongolia (Soviet troops remain), Chinese Eastern Railway; in exchange Sun accepts Soviet aid and United Front with the CCP (CCP members joining KMT as individuals). `[web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun-Joffe_Manifesto]`
- `[modern-scholarly-inference]` Anti-Soviet in economic doctrine, pro-Soviet in tactical alignment — a characteristic Sun move.

### 2.11 January 1924 — First KMT Congress Manifesto `[C]`
- `[documented]` Passed at First National Congress of the KMT, Guangzhou, 20–30 January 1924. `[web: http://www.republicanchina.org/KMT_First_National_Congress.pdf]`
- `[attested]` Mikhail Borodin (Soviet adviser, arrived Guangzhou October 1923) "prepared a constitution and helped draft a party program." `[web: https://www.britannica.com/place/China/Reorganization-of-the-KMT]`
- `[attested]` Wang Jingwei elected to Central Executive Committee, appointed Minister of Propaganda, with significant editorial influence. `[web: https://www.ebsco.com/research-starters/history/wang-jingwei]`
- `[modern-scholarly-inference]` Scholarly consensus (Fairbank, Bergère): collaborative — Sun's directives filtered through Borodin's organizational template plus Wang Jingwei / Hu Hanmin drafting. Sun retained final approval; radical anti-imperialist language bears Borodin's fingerprints. `[web: https://www.nybooks.com/articles/1981/05/28/his-man-in-canton/]`

### 2.12 January–August 1924 — 三民主義 Three Principles of the People lectures `[B]`
- `[documented]` 16 lectures at Guangzhou Higher Normal University, Jan–Aug 1924. Planned 18 lectures; last two (shelter, transportation) never delivered. `[web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three_Principles_of_the_People]`
- `[documented]` Transcription by **Huang Changgu** 黃昌穀 (notes) and **Zou Lu** 鄒魯 (manuscript review). (Zou Lu rendered "Tsui Lu" in older Wade-Giles.) `[web: http://www.taiwan-database.net/PDFs/WTFpdf21.pdf]`
- `[documented]` Standard English translation: Frank W. Price trans., L. T. Chen ed., *San Min Chu I: The Three Principles of the People* (Shanghai: China Committee, Institute of Pacific Relations, 1927). Da Capo reprint. `[web: https://archive.org/details/sanminchuthreepr0000drsu]` `[web: https://chinese.larouchepub.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/San-Min-Chu-I_ALL-en.pdf]`
- `[documented]` Structure: 6 lectures on 民族 (Jan–Feb), 6 on 民權 (Mar–Apr), 4 on 民生 (Aug). `[web: https://afe.easia.columbia.edu/ps/cup/sun_yatsen_democracy.pdf]`
- `[attested]` 民生 lectures show heavy citation of **Maurice William**. L. T. Chen (editor) said William's *The Social Interpretation of History* was Sun's "constant companion" during the lectures. `[web: https://international.ucla.edu/burkle/article/2813]`
- `[modern-scholarly-inference]` Huang-Zou transcription introduces editorial variation. Sun was alive during early publication; text is polished but stitched from stenographer's notes. The 1924 民族 lectures are **more anti-imperialist** and **less anti-Manchu** than Sun's pre-1911 output; the 民生 lectures are economically more radical yet paradoxically **less Marxist** after the Maurice William encounter.

### 2.13 January 1924 — 建國大綱 Fundamentals of National Reconstruction `[A]`
- `[documented]` 25 articles; adopted at First KMT Congress (passed 21 January 1924; promulgated 12 April 1924). `[web: https://chinacopyrightandmedia.wordpress.com/1924/04/12/fundamentals-of-national-reconstruction/]`
- `[documented]` Article 1: Three Principles + Five-Power Constitution as basis. Article 2: Minsheng — four needs (食衣住行). Article 3: Minquan — four rights (選舉罷免創制複決). Article 4: Minzu. `[web: https://chinacopyrightandmedia.wordpress.com/1924/04/12/fundamentals-of-national-reconstruction/]`
- `[documented]` Three stages locked in as KMT doctrine: 軍政 → 訓政 → 憲政. Stage 2: officials "trained and qualified through examinations" assist in preparing counties for autonomy. Stage 3 begins when >50% of provinces reach autonomy. `[web: https://chinacopyrightandmedia.wordpress.com/1924/04/12/fundamentals-of-national-reconstruction/]`
- `[modern-scholarly-inference]` KMT governing blueprint 1927–1947, basis of ROC constitution on Taiwan. `[web: https://sites.suffolk.edu/lawreview/2021/12/27/sun-yat-sens-san-min-doctrine-and-its-legacy-in-chinese-constitutionalism-three-principles-for-two-systems/]`

### 2.14 November 1924 — Kobe 大亞洲主義 Greater Asianism speech `[A]`
- `[documented]` Delivered 28 November 1924 at Kobe Prefectural Girls' High School. One of Sun's last major public speeches. `[web: http://japan-editor.blogspot.com/2013/12/blog-post.html]` `[web: https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Sun_Yat-sen's_speech_on_Pan-Asianism]`
- `[documented]` Contrasts Eastern 王道 (Kingly Way, rule of Right) with Western 霸道 (Hegemonic Way, rule of Might). Warns Japan against becoming "the watchdog of Western imperialism." `[web: https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Sun_Yat-sen's_speech_on_Pan-Asianism]`
- `[modern-scholarly-inference]` Chun-chieh Huang argues the speech has been contradictorily appropriated — by later Japanese pan-Asianists justifying Co-Prosperity Sphere, and by Chinese nationalists reading it as anti-imperialist warning. Chinese official text and contemporary Japanese-press text differ in key sentences. `[web: https://www.sciea.org/data-j03/06_Note_Chun-chieh%20Huang.pdf]`

### 2.15 March 11, 1925 — 總理遺囑 Three Testaments `[D]`

**A. Political Testament (to the KMT)**
- `[attested]` Drafted by Wang Jingwei; Sun signed as Soong Ching-ling guided his hand. `[web: https://ebrary.net/170976/history/sen_s_wills_testament_march_1925]`
- `[documented]` Famous closing: 革命尚未成功，同志仍須努力 "The revolution is not yet successful; my comrades must still exert themselves." `[web: https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Category:Last_Will_and_Testament_of_Sun_Yat-sen]`

**B. Family Testament (to Soong Ching-ling and children)**
- `[attested]` Drafted by Wang Jingwei. Brief. `[web: https://ebrary.net/170976/history/sen_s_wills_testament_march_1925]`

**C. Letter to Soviet Russia**
- `[attributed]` Historians and leaked party records: composed by Mikhail Borodin in English, translated into Chinese by Wang Jingwei who "adjusted the tone to resemble Sun's voice." Sun by then unable to hold a pen; Soong Ching-ling and Soviet operatives reported to have guided his hand. `[web: https://www.visiontimes.com/2026/03/14/how-a-soviet-adviser-ghostwrote-sun-yat-sens-pro-communist-deathbed-letter-in-1925.html]` `[web: https://www.yibao.net/2025/03/25/what-decrypted-soviet-archives-have-to-say-about-the-ccp-and-sun-yat-sens-ties-to-the-ussr/]`
- `[documented]` English text at Wikisource; calls on KMT to "fight shoulder to shoulder with Russia." `[web: https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Farewell_letter_to_Soviet_Russia]`
- `[modern-scholarly-inference]` **Preserve the contradiction.** Two views: (1) official CCP/KMT — Sun dictated and approved; (2) revisionist (Vision Times, Yibao) — Borodin fabrication signed in semi-conscious state. John K. Fairbank's 1981 *NYRB* review of Dan Jacobs's *Borodin* sits between — acknowledging Borodin's drafting while holding Sun approved the policy line. `[web: https://www.nybooks.com/articles/1981/05/28/his-man-in-canton/]`

## 3. Core Arguments — Recurring ≥ 3 Times

### 3.1 三民主義 — architecture and drift
- `[documented]` First formulated 1905 (Tongmenghui manifesto, *Minbao* inaugural editorial): 民族 / 民權 / 民生. `[web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three_Principles_of_the_People]`
- `[documented]` Sun equates with Lincoln's Gettysburg formulation: 民有 / 民治 / 民享 "of/by/for the people." Stated both in 1924 lectures and 建國大綱 context. `[web: https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E4%B8%89%E6%B0%91%E4%B8%BB%E7%BE%A9]`
- `[modern-scholarly-inference]` Shift in 民族: 1905 anti-Manchu 排滿 → 1912 五族共和 (Han/Manchu/Mongol/Tibetan/Hui) → 1924 single 中華民族 vs. Western imperialism. "The target of his nationalism had changed from the Manchus to the imperialist powers." `[web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_nationalism]`
- `[modern-scholarly-inference]` Shift in 民生: 1905–1912 primarily 平均地權 (Georgist); 1924 expanded to 節制資本 plus aggressive state industrialization plus post-William repudiation of Marx's class struggle.

### 3.2 五權憲法 Five-Power Constitution
- `[documented]` First proposed 1906 (Tongmenghui first-anniversary speech in Tokyo); restated 1916, 1919 孫文學說, 1924 lectures; anchored in Article 1 of 建國大綱. Adds 考試權 (exam) and 監察權 (control/censorate) to Montesquieu's three. `[web: https://www.britannica.com/event/Five-Power-Constitution]`
- `[documented]` Sun's framing: China "had two significant systems of examination and censoring in the past which could address shortcomings in Western government." `[web: https://www.britannica.com/event/Five-Power-Constitution]`
- `[attested]` Implemented 1928 under Chiang Kai-shek as the five-yuan structure. `[web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Control_Yuan]`

### 3.3 Three-stage revolution: 軍政 → 訓政 → 憲政
- `[documented]` 1905 Tongmenghui manifesto; 1918 essay "The Three Stages of Revolution" (Columbia Asia for Educators source); 1924 建國大綱 locks in as KMT doctrine. `[web: https://afe.easia.columbia.edu/ps/cup/sun_yatsen_revolution.pdf]`
- `[modern-scholarly-inference]` 訓政 tutelage justified single-party KMT rule 1928–1948; critics note structural parallels to PRC "democratic dictatorship." `[web: https://sites.suffolk.edu/lawreview/2021/12/27/sun-yat-sens-san-min-doctrine-and-its-legacy-in-chinese-constitutionalism-three-principles-for-two-systems/]`

### 3.4 知難行易 epistemological maxim
- `[documented]` Central in 孫文學說 (1919) Ch 1; restated in 1918 *Memoirs* and 1924 lectures — three appearances across corpus. Deliberately inverts 尚書 tradition and breaks with Wang Yangming's 知行合一. `[web: https://sunology.yatsen.gov.tw/detail/ad2bb3bd1762b20c3b066486ba0077e0/]`

### 3.5 天下為公 Tianxia wei gong
- `[attested]` Sun's most frequent calligraphic inscription. From 禮記·禮運: 大道之行也，天下為公. `[web: https://gaodawei.wordpress.com/2023/07/30/everything-for-the-common-good-tianxia-weigong-%E5%A4%A9%E4%B8%8B%E7%82%BA%E5%85%AC-sun-yat-sens-lifelong-pursuit-of-ideals-and-putting-them-into-practice/]` `[web: https://www.yatsen.gov.tw/en/News_Content2.aspx?n=6701&s=176079]`
- `[modern-scholarly-inference]` Sun's Confucian 大同 anchor — aligns republican politics with *Liyun* utopian ideal, claiming traditional sanction for overthrow of dynastic order.

### 3.6 平均地權 land equalization (Henry George)
- `[documented]` In 1905 Tongmenghui oath; Sun cites Henry George (*Progress and Poverty*, 1879) explicitly. `[web: https://www.prosper.org.au/geoists-in-history/sun-yat-sen-and-georgism/]`
- `[modern-scholarly-inference]` Wang (*AJES* 2026): Sun moves beyond George's tax-centric static model to a dual-track system (land to the tiller + public ownership of increment) fitted to semi-colonial agrarian China. `[web: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/ajes.70017]`
- `[documented]` Implemented: 1930 ROC Land Law; by 1995 Taiwan, 75.3% of municipal/prefectural tax revenue from land-value-increment + land-value taxes. `[web: https://progressandpoverty.substack.com/p/henry-george-and-sun-yat-sen-a-global]`

### 3.7 節制資本 / state-led industrialization
- `[documented]` Centerpiece of 實業計劃 (1921) and 1924 民生 lectures. State ownership/regulation of railways, ports, major industries; foreign capital welcomed under Chinese sovereignty. `[web: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Socialist_ideology_of_the_Kuomintang]`
- `[documented]` Sun called himself a "non-Marxian socialist"; state control of capital "would enable the entire people to share in capital's profits." `[web: https://taiwantoday.tw/Society/Taiwan-Review/23361/index]`

### 3.8 International assistance / peaceful development
- `[documented]` Dominant argument of *International Development of China* (1921) — Chinese peaceful rise through international cooperation; alternative to another European war.
- `[modern-scholarly-inference]` Sun's pitch failed at Paris (1919) and Washington (1921–22) conferences; this failure drives him toward Soviet alliance by 1923. `[web: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/17535654.2021.2100641]`

## 4. Intellectual Antecedents

- **Henry George** — cited from 1905 onward for 平均地權. `[web: https://www.prosper.org.au/geoists-in-history/sun-yat-sen-and-georgism/]`
- **Abraham Lincoln** — 1924 lectures: Gettysburg → 民有/民治/民享. `[web: https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E4%B8%89%E6%B0%91%E4%B8%BB%E7%BE%A9]`
- **Karl Marx** — cited to critique. Sun after Maurice William: "Marx was a social pathologist" who "mistook the phenomena he observed for the laws of social biology." Sun = "non-Marxian socialist." `[web: https://www.marxists.org/history/etol/newspape/ni/vol10/no05/leonard.htm]`
- **Maurice William** — Brooklyn dentist; *The Social Interpretation of History* (self-published, 2,000 copies). Sun cited him at KMT Congress Jan 1924 and through the 1924 lectures. William's later *Sun Yat-sen versus Communism* (1932) claims Sun as anti-Marxist. `[web: https://thechinaproject.com/2023/10/23/the-american-dentist-who-influenced-sun-yat-sen-and-his-nationalist-party/]` `[web: https://www.jstor.org/stable/2143360]`
- **Wang Yangming** — admired as activist; epistemology rejected. Cambridge *Review of Politics* "Wang Yang-ming and the Ideology of Sun Yat-sen." `[web: https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/review-of-politics/article/abs/wang-yangming-and-the-ideology-of-sun-yatsen/02442FC360B77BE147F39D698270D385]`
- **Confucian 大同 / Liji Liyun** — via 天下為公 signature. Kawata, *Confucianism and Sun Yat-sen's Views on Civilization*. `[web: http://www.princeton.edu/~collcutt/doc/Kawata_English.pdf]`

## 6. Phase-by-Phase Intellectual Shifts

| Phase | Years | Mode | Key texts | Doctrinal state |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Reformist physician | 1883–1894 | Reformist | 1894 Li Hongzhang letter | Wants Qing self-strengthening |
| Anti-Manchu revolutionary, Georgist | 1895–1911 | Revolutionary republican | Revive China Society oath 1894; *Kidnapped* 1897; *True Solution* 1904; *Minbao* 1905–07 | 驅除韃虜; 平均地權 |
| First-term provisional president | 1912 | Institutional | Provisional Constitution | 五族共和; abdication deal with Yuan Shikai |
| Wilderness / consolidation | 1913–1919 | Intellectual | 建國方略 (Parts 3, 1, then 2) 1917–1921; *Memoirs* 1918 | 知難行易 formulated |
| Rebuilding / Western disillusion | 1919–1923 | Economic-developmental | *International Development* 1921 | Seeks Western capital; spurned post-Versailles |
| Soviet alliance / final synthesis | 1923–1925 | Unified party-state blueprint | Sun-Joffe 1923; KMT First Congress + Manifesto 1924; Three Principles lectures 1924; 建國大綱 1924; Kobe speech 1924; Testaments 1925 | 聯俄容共扶助農工; 訓政 formalized |

- `[modern-scholarly-inference]` The late Sun is **more** economically radical (heavier state industrialization, "restriction of capital," Borodin-influenced mass line) yet **less** Marxist (via Maurice William) than middle-period Sun. The 1924 lectures hold both tensions simultaneously — central interpretive problem for any echo. `[web: https://www.wsp-publishing.com/en/article/doi/10.47297/wspctWSP2515-470201.20220601/]` `[web: https://link.springer.com/content/pdf/10.1057/9781403919755_8.pdf]`

## 7. Language and Translation

**Originally in English**: *Kidnapped in London* (1897, with Cantlie and Collins) · *True Solution* (1904) · *International Development* (1920–21) — Chinese 實業計劃 is a translation of Sun's English · *Memoirs of a Chinese Revolutionary* (1918 English edition).

**Originally in Chinese**: 上李鴻章書 (1894) · 孫文學說 (1919) · 民權初步 (1917) · 中國存亡問題 (1917) · 建國大綱 (1924) · 三民主義 lectures 1924 (oral → Huang/Zou transcript → Price English 1927) · 總理遺囑 Political + Family (1925).

**Japanese context**: Correspondence with Miyazaki Tōten (NDL archives); *Minbao* articles; Kobe Pan-Asianism speech 1924 (Sun spoke Chinese, translated live into Japanese by Dai Jitao 戴季陶; Japanese-press version and later Chinese "official" version differ). `[web: https://www.ndl.go.jp/portrait/e/datas/339]`

**Translation chain for English *Three Principles***: Spoken Mandarin (Sun, Guangzhou 1924) → Chinese stenographic notes (Huang Changgu) → Chinese revision (Zou Lu) → Chinese book → Frank W. Price translation with L. T. Chen editor → *San Min Chu I* (Shanghai: IPR China Committee, 1927) → Da Capo and later reprints.

## 8. Preserved Contradictions

1. **1925 Letter to Soviet Russia** — fully dictated by Sun vs. Borodin fabrication signed under duress. Both defensible.
2. **1924 KMT Manifesto** — Borodin-authored with Sun's approval (Bergère, Fairbank) vs. Sun-authored with Borodin edits (KMT orthodoxy).
3. **Wang Yangming — ally or target?** Yangmingism-continuator reading vs. 知行合一-opponent reading. Both textually defensible.
4. **1924 Sun more or less Marxist?** Borodin alliance suggests more; Maurice William suggests less. Answer: both simultaneously — institutionally left, doctrinally anti-Marxian.
5. **Three Principles text** — how much is Sun's wording vs. Huang/Zou polish? No raw stenographic notes published. Variant readings exist between printings.
6. **Kidnapped in London** — Anderson 2017 argues Collins's share has been understated in both Chinese and Anglophone scholarship.
